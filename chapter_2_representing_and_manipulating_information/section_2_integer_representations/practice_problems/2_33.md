# Practice 2.33

We can represent a bit pattern of length `w = 4` with a single hex digit. For a two's complement interpretation of these digits, fill in the following table to determine the additive inverses of the digits shown:

| Row     | Hex (x)     | Decimal (x) | Decimal (-x) | Hex (-x) 
| ------- |  ---------- | ----------- | -------- | ---------- 
| [A.](#a)| 0x2         |  ___        | ___      |  ___       
| [B.](#b)| 0x3         |  ___        | ___      |  ___     
| [C.](#c)| 0x9         |  ___        | ___      |  ___      
| [D.](#d)| 0xB         |  ___        | ___      |  ___      
| [E.](#e)| 0xC         |  ___        | ___      |  ___      

What do you observe about the bit patterns generated by two's complement and unsighed (Problem 2.28) negation?

---

## A.
```
x:
  0x2    
= 0b0010 
= 2      

-x:
  -0x2 
= ~0b0010
= 0b1101 + 1 
= 0b1110
= -2
= 0xE
```
